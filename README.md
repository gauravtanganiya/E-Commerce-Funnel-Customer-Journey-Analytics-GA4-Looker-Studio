Project Explanation: Google Merchandise Store – Stationery Funnel Analysis
1. Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing user behavior and business performance for the Google Merchandise Store, specifically within the stationery category. The analysis is structured across the marketing funnel:

Top of Funnel (TOFU): User acquisition and traffic sources
Middle of Funnel (MOFU): User engagement and interaction
Bottom of Funnel (BOFU): Conversions and revenue

The objective is to identify performance gaps, understand user behavior, and recommend strategies to improve conversions and revenue.
2. Top of Funnel (TOFU) – Traffic Analysis

Key Metrics:

Total Users: 14,390
Sessions: 16,895
Bounce Rate: 5.43%

Key Findings:

A large portion of traffic (around 48.8%) comes from direct sources, indicating strong brand awareness but also heavy dependency on a single channel.
Organic search contributes a moderate share, suggesting scope for SEO improvement.
Paid channels contribute minimally, indicating underutilization of performance marketing.
Desktop users dominate traffic, while mobile usage is comparatively low.
Traffic is geographically concentrated, primarily in the United States.

Interpretation:
The platform has strong brand recall but lacks diversification in acquisition channels and global reach.
3. Middle of Funnel (MOFU) – Engagement Analysis

Key Metrics:

Engaged Sessions: 15,977
Engagement Rate: 94.57%
Add to Cart: 5,389

Funnel Progression:

View Item: 100%
Add to Cart: 9.74%
Purchase: 1.41%

Key Findings:

Engagement levels are very high, indicating users are actively interacting with the platform.
There is a significant drop-off between product views and cart additions, and an even larger drop-off before purchase.
Desktop users have longer session durations compared to mobile users.
Engagement shows seasonal trends, with peaks around specific months.

Interpretation:
Users show strong interest, but there are inefficiencies in moving them from consideration to purchase.
4. Bottom of Funnel (BOFU) – Conversion and Revenue Analysis

Key Metrics:

Total Revenue: $202,025.10
Total Purchases: 778
Average Revenue per User: $2.01
Conversion Rate: 0.05

Key Findings:

Conversion rate is extremely low despite good engagement levels.
Revenue is highly dependent on direct traffic (47.9%) and organic search (24.4%).
Paid channels contribute relatively little to revenue.
A limited set of stationery products drives most of the revenue.

Interpretation:
The business generates revenue but is inefficient in converting traffic into paying customers, indicating major optimization opportunities.

5. Key Business Problems
Over-reliance on direct traffic as the primary acquisition channel
Low contribution from paid and organic growth channels
Significant drop-offs in the conversion funnel
Low mobile engagement and performance
Extremely low conversion rate despite high engagement
Limited product and revenue diversification
6. Recommendations
Channel Diversification
Invest in SEO to improve organic traffic
Increase spending on paid campaigns (Google Ads, performance marketing)
Expand into social media and referral channels
Conversion Rate Optimization
Simplify checkout flow
Improve product pages and CTAs
Use retargeting strategies for users who add items to cart
Mobile Optimization
Improve mobile UI/UX
Reduce load time and friction in navigation
Optimize checkout experience for mobile users
Customer Retention
Implement email marketing campaigns
Offer personalized recommendations
Introduce loyalty or repeat purchase incentives
Product Strategy
Expand beyond stationery products
Identify high-performing products and scale them
Target bulk or corporate buyers
7. Final Conclusion

The analysis shows that while the business has strong traffic and engagement, it struggles with conversion efficiency. The biggest opportunity lies in improving the conversion funnel, optimizing mobile experience, and diversifying acquisition channels to achieve sustainable growth.
